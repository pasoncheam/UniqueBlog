Stories from Nour Nader Nabil Salim


A Stressful Day


When I first arrived in Malaysia, it was right before the final exams of the first semester, so my first time on campus was actually not to attend an orientation meeting nor a regular lecture, but rather to sit for my first final exam. Of course you could imagine how stressed I was because of the exam, not to mention that it was my first time ever visiting the university and seeing it in real life. Plus, I was so nervous to finally meet my unimates. So basically, it really was not my day! But this was just the start of something worse, as I somehow got lost and could not reach the exam venue. Luckily, I reached the venue on time, but that wasn't just it. By the time I was preparing the stuff I would need in the hall such as my pencil case, the exam token and the student ID, I found a small tear in the lamination of my student ID, so I thought “Sure, why not remove the lamination?”. After I entered the exam hall and chose a spot to sit in, I looked at my student ID and it was all white just like a blank page. I panicked back then because the invigilators would need to see my picture on the ID to be able to sit for my exam, but then I realized that the picture was in the lamination that I just tore and threw away. I rushed to my bag and luckily found the lamination, but it was in a pretty bad condition. Luckily, I managed to tape it to the student ID again and sit for my exam. That whole day was a disaster and was so stressful back then, but now it is a story that gives me a good laugh every now and then. The moral of the story is: Do not, I repeat do not, tear the lamination on your student ID as it has your picture, ID number, and everything.
