Stories from Kee Huai Shen


ONLINE LEARNING


My case would be rather unique, as I am a local student but I have yet to visit UCSI for more than 10 times since the start of my foundation semesters. My home, which is in the Gombak area, is roughly 35 minutes from UCSI and yes, going back and forth between these 2 locations is very time consuming and placed quite a burden on both of my parents. If you had reviewed my groupmate, Pason’s story, I’m personally very amazed that he could attend the classes physically almost everyday. I do wish the rental fees surrounding UCSI would be lower, as that is the only issue I am facing in order to move out and stay in any accommodations that are near to UCSI. I have met and known most of my university friends through this social media platform called Discord, and many of them are really kind and friendly, which is a blessing to each of us freshmen. We are connected through games, anime and school work, and I will always stay with them whenever I visit UCSI. As for someone who rarely visits UCSI, having friends around when you visit is for sure, a great experience. As for online classes, sometimes you just can’t ignore the fact that sitting in your comfortable chair with the fan blowing on top of you makes your brain feel sleepy sometimes. I, for one that gets drowsy whenever I open a book to read, trying not to fall asleep and keeping focus during online classes are really challenging sometimes. As a solution, I listen to songs(on low volume of course), eat snacks or go for a walk around the room to get rid of the drowsiness. Attending online does come with a lot of perks too, the best example would be not needing to wake up 2 hours before the class starts, especially the days where there are classes during 8am, which is super painful for those who aren’t a morning person, like me. Another would be having the leisure to do your own task straight after the class ends, or just before the class starts. It is a very comfortable way of learning, but I myself, prefer attending physically if it weren't for the long distance between my house and UCSI.
