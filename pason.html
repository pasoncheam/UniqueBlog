Stories from Pason Cheam Tung Huay


MORNING HORROR



This is a personal experience I believe anyone who comes to campus earlier than 7 am can relate to. I live in an area around 30 minutes from the university campus and I would drive to university early in order to avoid traffic jams and be punctual to my 8 am classes. Every time I reached university, the sight was pitch black because the lights would only open automatically at 7 am which does not make sense to me since a lot of professors and lecturers arrive at school earlier and the sun will come out at around 7 am anyway. But moving on, the journey into the parking lot would be the scariest experience I have on days where I have classes. I usually go to block G on the 3rd floor to park my car so I will not get soaking wet when it rains heavily, and the parking floor below was reserved by vips. The parking before 7 am was totally dark, there were only a few of the lights on, the rest are only on when there is motion when a car drives below the lights. I am a skeptical person myself, but every time I get down my car, my human instincts kick in and I begin to feel scared as if there are unknown predators within my vicinity. Imagine if one of the motion sensor lights were on and there was no one there, how terrifying it would be! I would switch on my phone’s light and hold my metal water container to prepare myself for any “confrontations” if necessary until I reached the elevator to go to the ground floor. Still kind of annoyed that the university does not set the timer to switch on the lights at 6:30 am rather than 7 pm.
